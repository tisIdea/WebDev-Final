Why Tyler, The Creator's "IGOR" Changed Everything

Posted by Josephus | May 8, 2025

When Tyler, The Creator released "IGOR" in 2019, few could have predicted the seismic shift it would cause in hip-hop production. Six years later, its influence can be heard everywhere from mainstream pop to underground electronic music.

The album's combination of lo-fi aesthetics with lush, soulful arrangements created a template that countless artists have attempted to replicate. What makes "IGOR" so special is how it manages to feel both deeply personal and universally accessible.

EARFQUAKE
Tyler, The Creator

Steve Lacy's Rise: From iPhone Producer to Grammy Winner

Posted by Dea | May 5, 2025

Steve Lacy's journey from producing tracks on his iPhone to becoming a Grammy-winning artist is nothing short of inspirational. His minimalist approach to production has proven that creativity trumps technical resources every time.

With his unique blend of R&B, funk, and indie rock, Lacy has carved out a sound that's immediately recognizable. His latest album expands on this foundation while exploring new sonic territories that surprise and delight.

Bad Habit
Steve Lacy

Underground Artists You Need to Hear: Spring 2025 Edition

Posted by Josephus | April 28, 2025

Spring is in full bloom, and so is a new crop of underground artists pushing the boundaries of what music can be. From innovative production techniques to boundary-pushing lyrics, these artists represent the cutting edge of contemporary sound.

Our standout discovery this season is Mk. gee, whose ethereal productions and introspective lyrics have captivated our entire team. With just a handful of releases, he's already developing a signature sound that's as emotionally resonant as it is sonically adventurous.

Music has a unique ability to transport us back to specific moments in our lives. For team member Claire, these ten songs form the soundtrack to her formative college years, each one carrying memories of late-night study sessions, spontaneous road trips, and personal growth.
